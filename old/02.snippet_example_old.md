(intro.old)=
This week I finally found a clean pressing of the record I had been chasing
for years. The sleeve is worn at the corners, but the vinyl itself plays
beautifully from start to finish. [^condition.old] [^margin_note]

(playback.old)=
A long-playing record turns at 33⅓ revolutions per minute, so each revolution
takes about 1.8 seconds:

$$
T = \frac{60\ \text{s}}{33\tfrac{1}{3}} \approx 1.8\ \text{s} \label{eq_period.old}
$$

The stylus therefore sweeps past more slowly near the label than at the outer
edge — the linear velocity depends on the radius, and this equation is left
unlabeled on purpose, so it stays unnumbered:

$$
v = \frac{2\pi r}{T}
$$
