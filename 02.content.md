# Equations, figures, footnotes & comments

Content inside the panels behaves like any other MyST content — labels
resolve, equations and figures number, footnotes collect — but the plugin's
transforms apply the `.old`/`.new` contract so the two documents stay in
lockstep.

## Equations & figures

- Equations labeled with a shared stem — `\label{eq_period.old}` in the
  original, `\label{eq_period.new}` in the translation — display the **same
  number** in both panels, and each pair consumes exactly one slot in the
  page-wide sequence.
- Unpaired or unlabeled equations inside the panels are left **unnumbered**
  (the second equation below demonstrates this).
- Figures follow the same contract, sharing one caption number.
- Cross-references work against either side: @eq_period.old and
  @eq_period.new render the same number, as do @fig_cover.old and
  @fig_cover.new.

## Footnotes & comments

Footnotes follow the contract too:

- Matched labels — `[^condition.old]` ↔ `[^condition.new]` — are a translated
  pair: they share a number, tagged by side (**O.1** / **N.1**), and are listed
  in the **Footnotes** section at the bottom of the page.
- Footnotes *without* a counterpart are treated as translator **comments**:
  they number sequentially as **C.1, C.2, …** and collect in their own
  **Comments** section below the footnotes. In the demo below, the original
  carries an editorial comment and the translation a translator's note.
- Footnotes on the host page itself[^host] keep their plain numbers.
- Hover any marker for a popover; click it to jump to the entry.

[^host]: Like this one, written on the page rather than inside a panel.
